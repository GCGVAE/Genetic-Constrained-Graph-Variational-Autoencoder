About GCGVAE

In the past several months, COVID-19 has spread over the globe and caused severe damage to the people and the society. In the context of this severe situation, an effective drug discovery method to generate potential drugs is extremely meaningful. In this paper, we provide a methodology of discovering potential drugs for the treatment of Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome Corona-Virus 2 (commonly known as SARS-CoV-2). We proposed a new model called Genetic Constrained Graph Variational Autoencoder (GCGVAE) to solve this problem. We trained our model based on the data of various viruses’ protein structure, including that of the SARS, HIV, Hep3, and MERS, and used it to generate possible drugs for SARS-CoV-2. Several optimization algorithms, including valency masking and genetic algorithm, are deployed to ﬁne tune our model. According to the simulation, our generated molecules have great effectiveness in inhibiting SARS-CoV-2. We quantitatively calculated the scores of our generated molecules and compared it with the scores of existing drugs, and the result shows our generated molecules scores much better than those existing drugs. Moreover, our model can be also applied to generate effective drugs for treating other viruses given their protein structure, which could be used to generate drugs for future viruses.

Our Innovation

Combined Traditional and New Methods

GCGVAE model is consisted of genetic algorithm and constrained graph variational autoencoder. We combined these two algorithms to make sure our generate drug is novel and effective.

Using Neural Network to Sort Out Data

We trained an Edge Memory Neural Network (EMNN) to select useful molecules as the initial generation for the GCGVAE model. EMNN makes sure that all initial molecules are active and easy to produce.

Better Performance than Existing Drugs

We compared our generated molecules with existing drugs used for treating COVID-19 by calculating their binding affinity. Our results shows great improvements compared to existing drugs.

Could Be Reused to Generated Other Drugs

There are still many existing diseases caused by other viruses, like HIV etc. Our model could easily be adapted to generate effective drugs for other diseases given their virus’s protein structure.
